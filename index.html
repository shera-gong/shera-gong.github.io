About

I study how big data and emerging information technologies reshape firms’ disclosure decisions and capital allocation. My job market paper examines how consumer-sourced alternative data (e.g., NielsenIQ) on peer firms pressures “non-covered” firms to increase voluntary disclosure, with implications for market transparency and investor behavior.

Broadly, my research spans disclosure, information frictions, institutional investors, governance, and innovation. I build new measures from large-scale datasets (retail scanner data, regulatory filings, patents) to illuminate how information flows affect corporate decisions and market outcomes.

Research Snapshot

  • Job Market Paper: Forced to Talk? Spillover Effects of Alternative Data on Voluntary Disclosure

    Solo-authored. Uses NielsenIQ retail sales to construct a peer transparency measure and tests how “non-covered” firms respond in revenue forecasts and 8‑Ks.

    Key contributions: novel investor-driven mechanism; peer effects in disclosure; quasi-experiments using retailer expansions and institutional mergers.

    Details & draft

  • Alternative Data and Institutional Investor Trading

    Short- vs long-horizon investors’ responses to consumer-sourced and satellite data; effects on trading frequency and portfolio concentration.

  • Private Credit Transparency and Public M&A

    How BDC regulatory disclosures spill information into public markets and shape acquisition decisions of public firms.

  • Patent Disclosure, Collateral, and Innovation (AIPA 1999)

    Expanded patent transparency reduces valuation uncertainty, shifts collateral use and innovation choices toward more comparable assets.
