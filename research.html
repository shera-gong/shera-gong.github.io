Forced to Talk? Spillover Effects of Alternative Data on Voluntary Disclosure

Job Market Paper (solo-authored)

Consumer-sourced alternative data (NielsenIQ) make peer firms appear more transparent. I develop a continuous peer data exposure measure for “non-covered” firms and test whether they respond by issuing more frequent/timely revenue forecasts and adding forward-looking statements in 8‑Ks. Cross-sectional tests and quasi-natural experiments isolate an investor-driven pressure mechanism.

Alternative Data and Institutional Investor Trading

Solo-authored

Examines how short-horizon (transient) vs long-horizon (dedicated) institutions trade on alternative data (e.g., retail scanner data, satellite imagery). Documents changes in trading frequency and portfolio concentration.

Private Lending Disclosures and Public-Side Acquisition Decisions

With evidence from Business Development Companies (BDCs)

Shows how private-credit transparency in regulatory filings produces public signals used by acquirers, influencing private-target M&A outcomes.
